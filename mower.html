T Level Course Structure

T Levels are two-year courses designed for students aged 16-19, offering a blend of classroom learning and practical experience. Each T Level consists of three main components:

  • Core Component: Covers essential knowledge and skills relevant to the chosen industry or sector. This includes topics such as industry principles, theories, and practices.
  • Occupational Specialism: Allows students to specialize in a specific area within the industry, gaining deeper technical skills tailored to particular job roles.
  • Industry Placement: Involves a mandatory placement lasting at least 45 days, providing hands-on experience in a real-world setting to enhance practical skills and employability.

For more detailed information on T Level courses and their structure, visit the official UK government page on T Levels: Introduction of T Levels
